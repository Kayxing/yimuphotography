Our Mission

To raise public awareness of the Chinese and Asian LGBTQ+ women community to eliminate stereotypes, improve mental health, trigger social change.

Meet Our Board


Xiaoqian (Kay) Xing is a second-year student at Georgetown University McDonough School of Business. Prior to getting her MBA, Kay led teams in completing audits for Fortune 500 companies at Deloitte. Then, she started up her own music company, which has 36 employees and is the first LGBT-friendly company in the central area of China. After graduation, she will become an Amazonian.

She has several years of experience with accounting, operation, music tutoring, music post-production, music festival planning, and digital product launches. She is passionate about solving problems, developing strategies, analyzing data, and leading and inspiring people.


Yijia Gu is a first year graduate student at George Washington University.

After graduating from her undergraduate program, she worked in an operations position for a year, gaining experience in operations and developing a strong interest in technology. Later, she came to the United States to pursue further studies in full-stack technology. Her background helped her being a operation officer and web designer for YIMU.

She is also passionate about supporting LGBTQ+ rights, and hopes to use her skills and experiences to increase public awareness for minority women.

Meet Our Talents


Joyce Xu, is currently a director and cinematographer specializing in cultural and social impact documentaries, environmental films and commercials. She has 6 years of experience in international and multicultural film & TV production. The interview pieces of the documentary series about World War II, Vietnam war, Korea war, and Gulf War veterans, which she shot as a camera operator, has been and continues to be posted on the American Veterans Center's YouTube channel, with some videos reaching 510,000 views, and the whole work will also be available on Netflix as a web documentary.

The short documentaries, advertising, interviews, product videos, and promos filmed by her can also be accessed on the official websites and YouTube channels of local companies and organizations such as Earnest Medicine. Later this year, the environmental reality show “Break the Ice”, which she worked as an episode director, will be released on the NBC4 website. The good news is that the feature film “Daughter of Dunhuang” in which Joyce participated won the 35th annual Golden Rooster Award for Best Opera Film in China, and the Environmental film “Off The Grid” she worked as a Director of Photography is approaching completion!


Yi Wang (Historia) comes from Shanghai, China. She is a graduate from School of Visual Arts majored in Directing. She gains her bachelor degree of Translation from The Chinese University of Hong Kong (Shenzhen).

Historia is a producer, a director, a traveler and a team player. Her film works have been nominated in 9 international film festivals. For instance, The Perfect Smile, a stop motion animation directed and produced by her, has been nominated in Palm Springs International Animation Festival and Expo and Beijing International Film Festival.

Brave if, a 26 minute short film directed and written by her telling the struggle of a bisexual girl, is the May 2022 finalist of Oniros Film Awards. Also, Historia has been traveling around the world alone whenever she has the spare time since 13 and wishes to be a global citizen spreading happiness.

“Per aspera ad astra.”


Frances Siqi Fan (She/Her) is an image marker based in New York and Los Angels and an MFA candidate at CalArts Photography & Media. She graduated from the School of Visual Arts Photography & Videos in 2020.

Her current work direction is queer identity and self-censorship in Chinese society. Her works have been exhibited and screened internationally, including in New York, Milan, and Shanghai.

As a director, she also corroborated with fashion brands, including iDeat, Mini Bazaar, Kaleidos, Silky Miracle, Illamasqua, 依泉Uriage, 观夏SummerLab.
